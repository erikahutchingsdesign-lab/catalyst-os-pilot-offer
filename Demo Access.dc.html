How the time disappears

What 85–90% less production time actually looks like

Automated production, the repetitive build work (laying out, resizing, formatting, exporting) runs automatically instead of by hand, so hours of manual production collapse into minutes.
Fewer handoffs, no passing files between writer, designer, manager, and reviewer; the work moves through one app instead of stalling in five inboxes and three tools.
No status meetings or version chasing, one source of truth means no briefing calls, no “which file is current,” no rework from working off the wrong draft.
One person, end to end, a single person can carry a deliverable from brief to export, so a five-person, five-tool workflow becomes one person and one app, at the same quality.
No more tool tax

One app per task, so no one fights software built for someone else’s job

The traditional stack isn't the answer either, five tools, three handoffs, and someone whose whole job is keeping files straight. The software is the overhead, and bolting AI on top just speeds up the tangle. CatalystOS collapses it: one app per task, each person working in their craft and nothing else.

Writers write, no wrestling with design tools, mockups, or export settings.
Designers design, no reverse-engineering intent out of a Word doc or comment thread.
No file archaeology, one source of truth, versioned automatically. No “which file is current?”
No tool tax, fewer subscriptions, fewer licenses, fewer specialists hired just to run software.
Built in AI, not software

Your deliverables are built in AI, not trapped in files only a specialist can open

Traditional deliverables live inside the software that made them. The fact sheet is an InDesign file. The deck is a locked PowerPoint. The one-pager is a layered Figma doc. To change a number, you need the right license, the right software, and the one person who knows how to use it, and every edit spawns another version to track, name, and hope is the current one. CatalystOS builds deliverables in AI, as living digital assets. That changes what a deliverable is:

No proprietary software required, the asset isn’t chained to a $600-a-year license or the one specialist who owns it. It’s generated and updated in the platform.
Always current, never versioned by hand, there’s one source of truth. Update it once; the “final_v7_FINAL” file graveyard disappears.
Export to any format on demand, the same asset ships as PDF, PPTX, PNG, Word, or InDesign whenever you need it, without rebuilding it.
Owned, not rented, the deliverable is your design, your voice, your data. If a client ever leaves, they still walk away with a hard asset in hand.
Why Catalyst•OS

How is CatalystOS different?

A chat tool like Claude or ChatGPT knows nothing about your business and forgets everything when the session ends, so your team re-explains the brand, process, and approvals every time, and inconsistent output gets fixed by hand. Nothing accumulates.

CatalystOS does the remembering. Your brand, workflows, templates, and approvals live in the platform, so work comes out consistent and on-brand automatically. The model generates; CatalystOS makes it repeatable, reliable, and yours.

Built for agencies, not individuals, a raw model helps one person finish one task. CatalystOS puts your whole team on the same proven processes.
Remembers your business, instead of restarting with every prompt, it holds your brand, methodologies, templates, client history, and approval paths.
Locks in quality, every project runs through approved workflows, so output stays consistent across the org.
Wired into your stack, everything is created natively as code, so you’re not dependent on a stack of separate design tools. Then it exports to almost any format you need, Word, PowerPoint, Canva, Figma, and more, so the output drops right into wherever your work lives.
You don’t build or maintain it, the real alternative to CatalystOS isn’t “use Claude,” it’s building your own internal AI platform: versioning prompts, wiring integrations, governing quality, swapping models as they evolve. That’s a standing engineering function. CatalystOS is that platform, kept current for you.

The return

Reclaim the time, then bill for it

Two moves, one platform: the pilot gives your hours back, and everything after turns that freed capacity into recurring revenue. No number to publish, the math shows up in your own operation.

01 · During the pilot
Real work from month one
The pilot isn't a cost you evaluate at the end. It produces real client work from month one. The hours lost to handoffs, rework, and version chasing come back as finished output. Teams can get back more than they put in before the pilot ends, and everything you build, you keep.
02 · Beyond the pilot
The math inverts
Now you stop spending to save time and start earning on the capacity you freed. Each new client is added to the platform you already have, never rebuilt, so the same team serves more accounts. You grow by adding clients, not headcount, and every client makes the next cheaper to serve.
03 · The value, plainly
What you walk away with
The pilot repays you in reclaimed time. Beyond it, that time can become a recurring revenue source, more clients, same team, rising margin on each. You don’t buy software; you build an asset you keep and earn on. If you leave, you keep the hard assets. If you stay, your team retains access to the CatalystOS platform, and can create additional revenue by letting your own clients use the tools to update their materials.
04 · On pricing
Why the number isn’t the question
The real question isn’t the price, it’s what you already spend on strategizing processes that don’t work, multiple tools, rework, onboarding, and the hours lost between them. Measure the pilot against that, and against the revenue it opens, and the value is clear. Founding partners lock in early terms before market pricing is set.
